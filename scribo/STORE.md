Scribo is a side-by-side language journal: write in one language, watch a live translation appear as you type, and dig into any word without leaving the page. It is built for people learning a language who want to draft, translate, and study in one place.

**Highlights**

- Split editor with a markdown writing pane and a live translation pane that updates automatically about a second after you stop typing
- Double-click any word in the translation to pull up its phonetic spelling, parts of speech, definitions, examples, and back-translations in a popup
- One-click language swap that flips source and target text, plus a picker covering 23 languages including right-to-left support for Arabic
- Listen to the translation read aloud with streaming text-to-speech pronunciation
- Entries are saved locally with a per-entry translation cache, so revisiting work is instant and your journal stays on your machine
- Exposes its entries and translation engine as agent capabilities (list, search, create, update, delete, and translate markdown), so other tools and assistants can work with your journal

**Connects to:** DeepL for translations, ElevenLabs for spoken pronunciation, and FreeDictionaryAPI for word lookups. Your DeepL key is required; ElevenLabs is optional.

If you keep a journal to practice a language, Scribo turns each entry into a translation, a pronunciation, and a dictionary all at once.
