# People Ops — style guide

Warm, human people-and-culture review for HR and leadership audiences. The
bundled sample is a complete 18-slide year-in-people deck — tailor the org name,
teams, programs, and numbers.

- Palette: cream `#f7f3ec`, ink-sage `#2c352c`, sage-green `#4d7c5f` (primary),
  terracotta `#c4623f` (secondary / eyebrows). Type: display **Outfit** (Google),
  body **Lora** serif (Google). One accent plus the warm secondary — no gradients.
- Imagery: `people-ops-cover.jpg` (cover/close full-bleed, also reused as a team
  tile), `people-ops-fig.jpg` (culture split, also the team-grid montage). Warm,
  soft-focus editorial photography of diverse colleagues in calm light.
- Bespoke decoration:
  - `.divider/.divider-num/.divider-title/.divider-rule/.divider-sub` — cream
    section breaks with a sage rule and an italic subline.
  - `.hcband/.hc/.hc-num/.hc-label/.hc-delta` — the headcount stat band (hairline
    columns, terracotta up-deltas).
  - `.prog/.pcard/.pcard-ic(.warm)/.pcard-t/.pcard-d/.pcard-tag` — program cards
    with soft tinted icon chips (`.warm` flips the chip to terracotta).
  - `.teamgrid/.tg/.tg-scrim/.tg-cap/.tg-team/.tg-meta` — the team photo montage
    with a name plate.
  - `.voices/.voice/.voice-q/.voice-w` — pulse-survey quote cards with a leading
    quotation mark.
  - `.legend/.legend-row/.legend-dot` for donut/bar legends, `.note/.note-k` warm
    callout, `.lede` big intro line.
- Shared used: `.donut` (engagement score), `.bars` (what people value, attrition
  trend, DEI), `.table` (hiring & attrition), `.steps` (priorities), `.stats`,
  `.quote`, `.split.reverse`, `.full-bleed`, `.runner` footer on content slides.
- Arc: cover → our people in one line → headcount band → §Engagement → engagement
  donut → what people value (bars) → pulse voices → §Growth → hiring & attrition
  (table + bars) → learning & development (cards) → §Belonging → DEI progress
  (bars) → programs & benefits (cards) → culture split → §Ahead → priorities
  (steps) → team photo grid → culture quote → thank-you close.
- Density: keep numbers tabular and the tone warm and human — let the people lead
  and the sage/terracotta accents stay quiet. Reuse the two photos freely across
  the team grid; they crop cleanly via object-fit.
