# Project Kickoff — style guide

Energetic, friendly first-day deck for aligning a team on a new project before
work starts. The bundled sample is a complete 16-slide kickoff (the "Atlas
Onboarding Revamp") — tailor the project, owners, dates, and numbers.

- Palette: white `#ffffff`, deep-teal ink `#0f2e2b`, teal `#0d9488` (primary
  accent), coral `#f97316` (energetic secondary — corner tabs, eyebrows, "out"
  panel, bullets, risk callouts). Muted `#5b7570`. No gradients, lots of
  whitespace. Type: display **Outfit** (700/800), body **Inter**.
- Imagery: `project-kickoff-cover.jpg` (bright kickoff flat-lay — used for the
  cover and the closing quote), `project-kickoff-fig.jpg` (whiteboard
  collaboration — used for the ways-of-working `.split`). Section breaks are
  clean white `.divider`s with a coral number and teal rule.
- Bespoke: `.divider/.divider-num/.divider-title/.divider-rule`; `.goals/.goal`
  goal cards (coral corner tab + big teal numeral) for objectives; `.scope`
  with `.panel.in` (teal) / `.panel.out` (coral) for what is and isn't in
  scope; `.phase` pills dropped inside the shared `.flow` for the approach;
  RACI role chips `.rc-r/.rc-a/.rc-c/.rc-i` + `.raci-key` legend used inside a
  shared `.table`; `.cad/.cad-card` cadence cards for rituals & tools;
  `.risk/.risk-k` coral callout.
- Shared used: `.steps`, `.bullets`, `.checks`, `.flow`, `.timeline`, `.table`,
  `.donut`, `.stats`, `.split`, `.full-bleed`, `.quote`, `.runner`.
- Arc: cover → why we're here (problem/opportunity) → objectives & success
  (`.goals` + `.checks`) → §The plan → scope in/out (`.scope`) → approach
  (`.flow` phases) → milestones (`.timeline`) → §The team → RACI (`.table`) →
  ways of working (`.split` + `.steps`) → tools & cadences (`.cad`) → §Risks →
  risks & mitigations (`.table` + `.risk`) → north-star metric (`.donut` +
  `.stats`) → asks & decisions (`.checks`) → kickoff quote close.
- Density notes: keep dates and owners concrete — a kickoff lives on specifics.
  The `.runner` footer (brand left, section right) is on every content slide;
  drop it on the cover, dividers, and close. Coral is a seasoning, not a base —
  reach for it on one element per slide, never as a fill behind text.
